Our history

A BRIEF HISTORY OF THE SCHOOL

The Universal Basic Education Commission (UBEC) model smart schools in Nigeria were established through a partnership initiative with the Korean International Cooperation Agency (KOICA), launched in 2021. The smart school initiative was a direct response to poor educational outcomes, a high number of out-of-school children, and a lack of digital literacy among both students and teachers in Nigeria. A 2021 assessment by UBEC and KOICA highlighted that over 10.1 million school-age children were out of school, and more than 60% of public primary school teachers lacked basic digital skills. Strategic partnerships To implement the program was made, UBEC signed a Memorandum of Understanding (MOU) with KOICA in October 2021. The partnership brought in foreign investment and expertise, with KOICA providing a $10 million grant for the project's first phase. Although the KOICA-funded phase focused on six pilot schools, UBEC independently and with other partners developed a broader plan to build a model smart school in each of Nigeria's 36 states and the Federal Capital Territory (FCT).

Mission: This program was designed to improve digital learning in public basic education across the country.
